Academic Background

I hold two Bachelor's Degrees from Universidad Carlos III de Madrid (UC3M): one in Telematics Engineering and another in Computer Science Engineering, both completed in full English. Additionally, I have a Master’s Degree in Information Systems from Universidad Rey Juan Carlos (URJC), where I graduated with an outstanding average grade of 9.07. Pursuing these degrees independently and simultaneously allowed me to build a strong foundation in telecommunications, network architectures, software development, and data systems. These studies, have been instrumental in equipping me with the expertise to contribute to cutting-edge research, including my current PhD work focused on 6G technologies.

Researcher in Deterministic Networking and 6G

I am currently a PhD Researcher at the Telematics Department of Universidad Carlos III de Madrid (UC3M). My research focuses on the design and development of Time-Sensitive Networking (TSN) solutions for next-generation communication architectures, including 6G networks. I specialize in creating deterministic multi-domain network environments by integrating 3GPP, Wi-Fi, and Ethernet technologies. My work includes predictive modeling for network performance, implementing TSN 802.1Q and DetNet standards, and developing OpenAPI specifications for inter-domain management services. These efforts contribute to enhancing low-latency communication, deterministic data delivery, and intelligent orchestration in future telecommunication systems.

Expert in Programable Networks

I have extensive experience in Python, Golang, and C and many others, with a strong focus on low-level programming and under-kernel technologies. My expertise includes working with advanced network programming frameworks such as AF_XDP and eBPF to develop high-performance, programmable network solutions. I specialize in creating and optimizing custom network functionalities, leveraging these technologies to achieve low-latency processing and efficient resource management. My work also involves integrating programmable networking paradigms with Software-Defined Networking (SDN) frameworks and implementing OpenAPI standards to enable seamless and intelligent network orchestration.
